Applicant Journey

the full admissions process — from signup to program onboarding

Applicants progress through a defined pipeline with 7 major stages. The journey is tracked in the applicant_stage table, which holds both the current_stage (where they are in the process) and program_admission_status (their acceptance outcome). Each stage unlocks new actions on the applicant dashboard.

2 — Stage-by-Stage Breakdown
1
Account Creation signup
/apply/signup
The applicant creates an account with their name, email, and password. A verification email is sent. They must verify before accessing the dashboard. Referral source and NYCHA residency are captured.
Verified → Dashboard Unverified → Blocked
2
Info Session info_session_registered
/info-sessions
The applicant browses available info sessions and registers for one. Sessions can be in-person (at Pursuit HQ in Long Island City) or online. Capacity is tracked. Attendance is required before the application will be considered.
Attended No Show Registered
3
Application application_submitted
/application-form
Multi-section form with auto-save (1-second debounce). Includes an eligibility check that validates age, income, location, work authorization, commute ability, and schedule availability. If ineligible, the application is immediately marked as such and a rejection email is sent. If eligible, the application proceeds to submission. Progress is tracked across sections.
Submitted Ineligible Draft / In Progress
4
Review & Workshop workshop_invited
/workshops
After submission, the application undergoes AI analysis (scoring for learning aptitude, grit, and critical thinking) followed by human review. Staff use deliberation flags (yes/maybe/no) to decide. Eligible applicants are invited to a workshop. The workshop card on the applicant dashboard unlocks. Applicants select a timeslot and indicate laptop needs. The "Enter Workshop" action converts the applicant to a builder account and grants access to the workshop learning environment.
Workshop Attended No Show Rejected Waitlisted
5
Program Decision program_admission_status
After the workshop, staff make a final admission decision. This is stored separately from the stage as program_admission_status. Possible outcomes: accepted, rejected, waitlisted, withdrawn, or deferred (rolled to the next cohort). Deferred applicants re-enter the pipeline at the workshop invitation stage for the next cohort.
Accepted Rejected Waitlisted Deferred
6
Pledge pledge_completed
/pledge
Accepted applicants complete a 6-step pledge wizard: Introduction, Learning commitments, Community commitments (with Code of Conduct acknowledgment), Building commitments, Personal Information (name, email, phone, DOB), and a Signature (canvas-based with touch support). The pledge card replaces the workshop card on the dashboard.
Pledge Completed Pending
7
Onboarding onboarding
/onboarding
After the pledge, the onboarding card appears on the dashboard (replacing the pledge card). 8 tasks must be completed: Program Details & Requirements, Attendance Policy & Calendar, Join Slack, Set Up Pursuit Email, Google Calendar, Download Kisi, Building in Public, and Engage with Tech News. When all required tasks are done, the applicant can create their builder account and transition to the main application as a full member.
Builder Account Created In Progress
3 — Decision Branches

Ineligible at Application Stage

If the eligibility check fails (age, income, location, work auth, commute, or schedule), the application status is set to ineligible and a rejection email is sent. The applicant can edit their eligibility responses and re-check. A full reapply option exists for previously rejected applicants.

Deferred to Next Cohort

Staff can defer an applicant to a future cohort. The deferred flag is set, along with deferred_from_cohort_id. Rollover tracking records the cohort history (rollover_cohorts array, rollover_count). When the next cohort opens, deferred applicants re-enter at the workshop invitation stage.

No Show Recovery

If an applicant no-shows an info session or workshop, their stage is updated accordingly (info_session_no_show or workshop_no_show). Info session no-shows can re-register for a different session. Workshop no-shows typically require manual intervention by staff.

4 — Stage Values (applicant_stage.current_stage)
info_session_registered
Signed up for an info session, hasn't attended yet
info_session_attended
Attended an info session, ready to apply
info_session_no_show
Registered but did not attend info session
application_submitted
Application submitted, awaiting review
application_under_review
Under AI + human review
workshop_invited
Invited to attend a workshop
workshop_registered
Registered for a specific workshop timeslot
workshop_attended
Attended the workshop, awaiting program decision
workshop_no_show
Registered but did not attend workshop
5 — Program Admission Statuses
pending
Default state. No decision has been made yet.
accepted
Admitted to the program. Unlocks pledge and onboarding.
rejected
Not admitted. Can reapply for a future cohort.
waitlisted
On hold. May be admitted if spots open.
withdrawn
Applicant chose to leave the process.
